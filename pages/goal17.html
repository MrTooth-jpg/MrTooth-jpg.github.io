Goal 17: Partnerships for the Goals

Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development by 2030.

Why are Partnerships Important?

Global challenges such as poverty, inequality, and climate change require a unified approach that brings together governments, businesses, civil society, and individuals. Partnerships allow for shared knowledge, resources, and expertise, fostering collaborative efforts that can address issues on a larger scale. Through partnerships, we can achieve the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and ensure a more prosperous and sustainable future for all.

Key Facts

  • Collaboration across sectors and regions is essential to achieving the SDGs, as no single organization or country can tackle these global challenges alone.
  • Official development assistance (ODA) remains vital for supporting developing countries in achieving the SDGs.
  • Public-private partnerships can play a significant role in mobilizing resources and scaling up solutions for sustainable development.

How Can We Achieve This Goal?

  • Promote and support partnerships at all levels, from local to global, to foster collaboration across sectors.
  • Enhance international cooperation, especially in areas such as technology transfer, innovation, and financing for sustainable development.
  • Encourage the private sector to take a more active role in sustainable development through corporate social responsibility (CSR) and impact investing.
  • Support multilateral institutions, such as the United Nations, in facilitating global partnerships and dialogue for development.
  • Ensure that the voices of marginalized groups, including youth, women, and indigenous communities, are included in partnership discussions and decision-making processes.
