Goal 6: Clean Water and Sanitation

Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all by 2030.

Why is Clean Water and Sanitation Important?

Access to clean water and sanitation is essential for human health and well-being. It is necessary for the survival of all life, human and animal, and is crucial for reducing diseases, improving hygiene, and ensuring the overall sustainable development of communities and countries.

Key Facts

  • Nearly 2.2 billion people around the world lack access to safely managed drinking water services.
  • Over 4.2 billion people do not have access to safely managed sanitation.
  • Water scarcity affects more than 40% of the global population, and this number is expected to increase with climate change.

How Can We Achieve This Goal?

  • Improve water quality by reducing pollution, eliminating dumping, and minimizing the release of hazardous chemicals and materials.
  • Increase water-use efficiency across all sectors and implement integrated water resources management at all levels.
  • Invest in modern, efficient, and sustainable water and sanitation infrastructure, particularly in rural and underserved areas.
  • Encourage water conservation and sustainable practices in communities, businesses, and industries.
  • Ensure equitable access to water and sanitation, particularly for marginalized and vulnerable populations.
