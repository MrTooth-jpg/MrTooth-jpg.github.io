Goal 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth

Promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all by 2030.

Why is Decent Work and Economic Growth Important?

Decent work and economic growth are essential for building a strong economy, reducing poverty, and ensuring a better quality of life. This goal focuses on creating conditions where people can access productive and meaningful employment, fair wages, and a safe working environment, contributing to the prosperity of both individuals and society.

Key Facts

  • Globally, 71 million young people are unemployed, and many more are underemployed or working in informal jobs.
  • Nearly 1.3 billion people live on less than $1.25 a day, without access to basic services.
  • The global economy is growing slower than necessary to achieve full employment and decent work for all.

How Can We Achieve This Goal?

  • Promote policies that encourage entrepreneurship, particularly among youth and marginalized groups.
  • Increase investments in infrastructure, including green and sustainable industries, to create jobs.
  • Ensure that all workers have access to fair wages, safe working conditions, and social protection.
  • Support the transition to a more sustainable and circular economy that reduces environmental impact while creating quality jobs.
  • Foster skills development and lifelong learning to help workers adapt to changes in the labor market and technology.
